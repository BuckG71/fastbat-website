About FASTBAT

Who We Are

Our Mission

FASTBAT — Friends and Supporters of Trinity Baseball — exists to enrich the Trinity University baseball experience. We rally parents, alumni, fans, and the San Antonio community around our Tigers.

We provide support that goes beyond the university budget: travel funding, equipment, team events, and scholarships for student-athletes who represent Trinity with pride.

As a registered 501(c)3 nonprofit, every dollar you contribute is tax-deductible and goes directly to the program. 501(c)3 Verified

Welcome to the Alamo City

Visit San Antonio

Traveling to see the Tigers? San Antonio is one of the most vibrant cities in Texas — here's our short list of must-sees for families visiting from out of town.

⭐

Must-See

The Alamo
The iconic symbol of Texas independence. Free admission, right in the heart of downtown — a must for first-time visitors.
The River Walk
Three miles of riverfront dining, shopping, and strolling along the San Antonio River. Magical at night.
The Pearl District
A renovated brewery complex turned foodie and arts destination. Weekend farmers market is a local highlight.
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Family-Friendly

San Antonio Zoo
One of the top-ranked zoos in the country. A full day of fun for families of all ages.
Natural Bridge Caverns
Spectacular underground caverns just 20 minutes north of campus. A true Texas treasure.
Six Flags Fiesta Texas
The local theme and waterpark — great if you're extending your visit over a full weekend.
🍽️

Great Eats

Tex-Mex
You're in San Antonio — it's non-negotiable. Try Mi Tierra (open 24 hrs!) or Rosario's on South Alamo.
BBQ
Two Bros. BBQ Market is a local favorite. Get there early — they sell out. Worth every minute of the wait.
Breakfast
Magnolia Pancake Haus near the Medical Center. Legendary weekend brunch — bring your appetite.
🚗

Day Trips

New Braunfels & Gruene
45 min north — tubing on the Comal River and Gruene Hall, the oldest dance hall in Texas.
Fredericksburg
90 min west into the Hill Country. Wineries, German heritage, and stunning wildflowers in spring.
Wimberley
A charming artists' village on the Blanco River. Blue Hole Regional Park is a beloved local swim spot.
